## Description

Turn handwriting, numeral recognition, spelling and maths practice into a space adventure.

Maxi's Mighty Mindgames brings together four playful learning experiences for children aged 6–8. Short missions, calm feedback and meaningful rewards help learners keep going without turning every mistake into a noisy interruption.

DOT-TO-DOT DISCOVERY

Recognise numerals while revealing 84 hand-reviewed, child-friendly illustrated pictures. Every visible dot belongs to the picture, and every exterior turn is one the numbered trail can actually draw.

• Choose Tap dots to press the next numeral, or Trace lines to draw from the last dot to the next
• Build confidence across numeral ranges 1–10, 1–15 and 1–20, with selected 1–25 adventures
• See and hear the next numeral, with a gentle hint if needed
• Scan the whole field: varied spacing makes the numeral more useful than simply choosing the nearest dot
• Zoom into the finished artwork and colour meaningful regions using picture-specific colours
• Choose quick tap-to-shade or colour directly with a finger or Apple Pencil
• Build subitising confidence with standard 1–5 dot patterns and a quick Star Spot bonus
• Occasionally move into Comet Writer to form a numeral between pictures
• Explore animals, vehicles, food, nature and everyday favourites

COMET WRITER

Practise all 26 lowercase letters, 26 capitals and the numbers 0–9. Every symbol has a clear starting point, stroke order and direction, with writing-line guidance for ascenders, x-height, baseline and descenders.

• Follow the glow, connect the stars, then write independently
• Get immediate feedback on starting point, direction, path and line placement
• See a formation score out of 100 and earn Comet Points for every attempt
• Pick any symbol instantly in Quick Practice
• Recall chosen letters from sight or by hearing their letter names
• Hear ordinary letter names and write the match in Letter Name Mission
• Write a whole common word on one continuous writing line
• Build control first with Flight School's lines, curves, slants and loops
• Move from the screen to real pencil-and-paper practice
• Choose finger input or Apple Pencil-only input on compatible iPad models
• Adjust letter size and turn writing lines on or off

For the most comfortable handwriting experience, Comet Writer is best played with a capacitive stylus.

STAR SPELLER

Choose a Year 1 phonics or tricky-word group and a 3, 5 or 10-word mission. The child hears each hidden word in a short context sentence, types it and then forms every letter using Comet Writer's guided handwriting tools. Adaptive practice revisits spellings that needed errors or hints, and the active child profile keeps a grown-up progress summary.

A grown-up can add a familiar word list with optional example sentences. Optional pronunciations recorded by a grown-up remain on the device.

GROWN-UP MISSION CONTROL

Create a separate profile for each child, choose who is playing when the app opens and switch from the main menu. Each profile keeps its own progress. When iCloud is available, profile details and a small progress snapshot sync privately between devices using the same Apple Account.

Grown-ups can also choose left- or right-handed writing, see formation mastery, replay saved writing trails, make a local PDF progress report, add custom practice words and optionally record a pronunciation on the device.

CIRCUIT CHALLENGE

Solve the glowing hex, then follow the connector carrying its answer. Play a gentle adventure or customise a quick game with addition, subtraction, multiplication and division. Choose the exact times tables to practise, change the number range and grid size, or create printable puzzles for offline play.

BUILT FOR FOCUSED PRACTICE

• No adverts
• No in-app purchases
• No developer account or separate sign-in
• No third-party analytics or tracking
• Works offline; private Apple iCloud progress sync resumes when available

A grown-up gate protects detailed progress tools, reports, custom words, profile deletion and optional microphone recording. Writing trails, custom words and voice recordings stay on the device. A grown-up can explicitly export a local report through Apple's system share sheet.

## App Review notes

Version 1.4 adds Dot-to-Dot Discovery, Star Speller and private progress sync through Apple's NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore. No developer account, payment or network connection is required. The app contains no adverts, in-app purchases, third-party analytics or tracking SDKs.

Suggested review path:

1. On the hub, open Dot-to-Dot Discovery. Both requested styles are visible at the top: Tap dots (“Press the next numeral”) and Trace lines (“Draw from the last dot to the next”).
2. In Tap dots, open Giraffe and press 1, 2, 3 in order. In Trace lines, open Sailboat, press 1 to begin, then drag from 1 to 2. Tapping 2 without tracing produces a calm reminder and does not advance.
3. Complete Giraffe, then open its full-screen colouring stage. Switch between Tap and Pencil modes: the available colours and masks correspond to the giraffe, spots and background rather than generic strips. Completing every colour shows the reward; More Pictures returns directly to the gallery. A separate Star Spot prompt remains optional.
4. Return to the gallery and choose Big Number Adventure to see selected 1–25 trails such as Ocean Ship, Dump Truck and Scooter. Every visible exterior turn corresponds to a numbered dot; there are no fake dots.
5. Return to the hub, tap the round profile avatar, then Add profile. With multiple profiles, the “Who’s playing?” picker appears on launch and the active player can be changed from the hub or Settings.
6. Open Comet Writer. The capacitive-stylus recommendation appears at the top. Open Letter Name Mission to hear the target letter's ordinary name using Apple's on-device speech synthesizer.
7. Open Star Speller, choose a Year 1 group and select a 3, 5 or 10-word mission. Built-in words are spoken in authored context sentences. A grown-up can use My Words behind the random multiplication gate to add or import words and optional example sentences. Microphone access is requested only if Record is explicitly chosen.
8. Open Circuit Challenge, then Quick Play and Custom Settings. Enable multiplication or division to see the individual times-table picker.

iCloud implementation: the app stores profile metadata and a compact merge-only progress envelope in NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore so the same Apple Account can continue across devices. There is no developer server. Writing traces, detailed attempts, custom words and optional audio recordings are excluded from iCloud and remain on-device. Existing users' progress is migrated into their first profile. Clear All Data writes a reset marker so an older device cannot restore deleted progress.

A grown-up may explicitly export a locally generated progress report or puzzle through Apple's system share sheet. Pencil-only input is shown only on iPad, where Apple Pencil is available.

## App privacy answers

- Does this app or its third-party partners collect data? No. Retain `DATA_NOT_COLLECTED` because the developer has no server or access to the private iCloud value; Apple's guidance says developers are not responsible for disclosing data collected by Apple.
- Tracking: No.
- Tracking domains: None.
- Third-party SDKs in the iOS target: None.
- Private Apple service: profile names, hand preference and a compact progress snapshot are saved through `NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore` for the same Apple Account.
- Data processed only on device: detailed writing traces and attempts, custom words and optional audio recordings.
- Permission: Microphone, optional and requested from the adult-gated My Words feature only.

Apple says data it collects through an Apple framework is not the developer's disclosure unless the developer collects that data from the framework. Keep the answer as Data Not Collected only while the developer has no backend access and the implementation remains limited to Apple's private per-account key-value store.

## Export compliance

Version 1.4 build 6 contains no custom cryptography and declares `ITSAppUsesNonExemptEncryption = false`. Its only off-device data path is Apple's system-provided iCloud key-value service. If App Store Connect asks, answer that the app does not use non-exempt encryption. Re-audit before any release that adds custom cryptography, a developer backend or a third-party SDK.
